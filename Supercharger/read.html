Supercharger (10 Marks)

Quick Glance

A system that delivers oxygenated air into the combustion chambers of an engine, enhancing engine performance by increasing air density and improving combustion efficiency.

Core Concepts

Function

Delivers clean, continuous air to the engine while managing intake noise and temperature.

Working

Driven by engine pulley through V belt, operating at high speeds up to 80,000 RPM.

Purpose

Increases air density and engine power output while compensating for altitude power loss.

Working Process

Engine Drive

Takes power from engine pulley

Air Compression

Impeller spins at high speeds

Air Intake

Compressed air enters chamber

Combustion

Enhanced air-fuel mixture

Power Output

Increased engine performance

Advantages & Limitations

Advantages

  • Instant power delivery
  • Simple installation
  • Reliable performance
  • No turbo lag
  • Better low-end torque

Limitations

  • Consumes engine power
  • Increases engine strain
  • Higher noise levels
  • Increased engine size
  • Detonation risk in SI engines
