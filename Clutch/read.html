Clutch (10 Marks)

Quick Glance

A clutch is a mechanical device that engages and disengages power transmission between driving and driven parts of a vehicle. It enables smooth power transfer and gear changes while protecting the transmission system.

Core Concepts

Role

Mediates between engine shaft and gearbox, allowing smooth power transmission and gear changes.

Principle

Works on friction between surfaces, enabling controlled power transfer between rotating shafts.

Purpose

Enables smooth engagement/disengagement of power, protects transmission, and facilitates gear changes.

Working Process

Pedal Release

Clutch plates engage

Engagement

Friction surfaces contact

Power Transfer

Torque transmission begins

Full Contact

Complete power transfer

Advantages & Limitations

Advantages

  • Smooth power transmission
  • Facilitates gear changes
  • Protects transmission system
  • Enables gradual engagement
  • Reduces driveline shock

Limitations

  • Wear and tear over time
  • Regular maintenance required
  • Heat generation during slipping
  • Limited friction material life
